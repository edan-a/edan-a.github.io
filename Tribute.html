Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln was an American statesman and lawyer who served as the 16th president of the United States.

He was born near Hodgenville, Kentucky on February 12, 1809, and his family was poor and he grew up on the edge of the frontier in Indiana.

After moving away from home, Lincoln co-owned a general store for several years before selling his stake and enlisting as a militia captain defending Illinois in the Black Hawk War of 1832.
